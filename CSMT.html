Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus

The Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus, located in Mumbai, Maharashtra, is an iconic railway station.

Established in 1887, the Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus features Victorian Gothic and Indo-Saracenic Revival architecture.

Functioning as a major railway station, it was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2004.

The main building, dome, and turrets are notable structures within the station complex.

With its architectural splendor and historical significance, it attracts tourists and commuters alike.

The station, formerly known as Victoria Terminus, was renamed in honor of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1996.
