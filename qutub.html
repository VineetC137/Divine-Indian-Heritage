Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the tallest brick minaret in the world.

Established in 1193, Qutub Minar is a masterpiece of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Functioning as a minaret, it is a major tourist attraction and symbolizes Delhi's rich history.

Notable structures within the complex include the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque and the Iron Pillar of Delhi.

Qutub Minar was built by Qutb-ud-din Aibak and expanded by successive rulers.

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1993, it represents the beginning of Indo-Islamic architecture in India.
