Khajuraho Group of Monuments

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments, located in Madhya Pradesh, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its intricate sculptures and Nagara-style Hindu temple architecture.

Established between 950-1050 CE, the Khajuraho temples are a religious and ceremonial complex.

Notable structures include the Kandariya Mahadeva Temple and Lakshmana Temple, showcasing exquisite craftsmanship.

Visitors are attracted to the intricate sculptures, including the famous erotic carvings, and the spiritual ambiance of the site.

Built by the Chandela dynasty rulers, these monuments represent the pinnacle of Hindu temple architecture and sculpture.

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1986, Khajuraho celebrates love, life, and spirituality through its intricate carvings.
