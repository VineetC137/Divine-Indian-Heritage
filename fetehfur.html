Fatehpur Sikri

Fatehpur Sikri, located in Uttar Pradesh, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its Mughal architecture and historical significance.

Established in 1571, Fatehpur Sikri showcases Mughal architecture with Indo-Islamic elements.

Functioning as the capital of the Mughal Empire, notable structures include the Buland Darwaza, Jama Masjid, and Panch Mahal.

Tourists are drawn to its historical significance, architectural splendor, and cultural heritage.

Founded by Emperor Akbar, Fatehpur Sikri was abandoned after 14 years due to water scarcity despite its grandeur.

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1986, it represents a fusion of Hindu and Islamic architectural styles.
