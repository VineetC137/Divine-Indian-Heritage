Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace, located in Mysore, Karnataka, is a symbol of opulent architecture and royal heritage.

Originally built in the 14th century, the current structure of the Mysore Palace was completed in 1912.

The palace features Indo-Saracenic Revival architecture and served as a royal residence and administrative center.

While not designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Mysore Palace is one of India's most visited attractions.

Notable for its Ambavilas Palace, the Mysore Palace attracts tourists with its architectural beauty and grandeur.

It is renowned for its light and sound show, adding to the visitor's experience of its opulence.
