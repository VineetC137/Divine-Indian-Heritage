Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture and a symbol of eternal love.

Constructed between 1632 and 1653, the Taj Mahal exemplifies the fusion of Indian, Persian, and Islamic architectural styles.

Functioning as a mausoleum, it houses the white marble tomb of Mumtaz Mahal, the beloved wife of Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan.

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983, the Taj Mahal attracts millions of visitors annually from all over the globe.

The white marble mausoleum is the most notable structure within the complex, symbolizing eternal love and architectural brilliance.

It is revered as one of the Seven Wonders of the World and remains a significant cultural and historical landmark.
