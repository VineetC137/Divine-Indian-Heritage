Konark Sun Temple

Konark Sun Temple, located in Odisha, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its architectural splendor and religious significance.

Established in the 13th century, Konark Sun Temple showcases Kalinga architecture.

Functioning as a temple dedicated to the Sun God, its notable structure includes the chariot-shaped sanctum and intricate sculptures.

Tourists are drawn to its architectural splendor, intricate stone carvings, and religious significance.

History reveals that it was built by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty.

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1984, it symbolizes Odisha's rich cultural heritage and craftsmanship.
