Jaipur City Palace

The Jaipur City Palace, located in Jaipur, Rajasthan, is a magnificent example of Mughal and Rajput architecture.

Established in 1729, the Jaipur City Palace serves as a royal residence and administrative center.

Its architecture is a blend of Mughal and Rajput styles, reflecting the rich cultural heritage of Rajasthan.

While not designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it remains a well-maintained heritage site.

The Chandra Mahal and Mubarak Mahal are notable structures within the palace complex.

Visitors are drawn to its museums, courtyards, and historical artifacts, showcasing Rajput culture.
