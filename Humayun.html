Humayun's Tomb

Humayun's Tomb, located in Delhi, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its architectural splendor and historical significance.

Established between 1572-1575, Humayun's Tomb features Mughal architecture with Persian influences.

Functioning as the tomb of Emperor Humayun, notable structures include the main tomb and Char Bagh garden complex.

Tourists are drawn to its architectural splendor, lush gardens, and historical significance.

Commissioned by Humayun's wife, Empress Bega Begum, it is considered a prototype of Mughal mausoleums.

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1993, it influenced subsequent Mughal monuments, including the Taj Mahal.
