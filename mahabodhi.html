Mahabodhi Temple

Mahabodhi Temple, located in Bodh Gaya, Bihar, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the holiest site in Buddhism.

Established in the 3rd century BCE, the current structure of Mahabodhi Temple dates back to the 5th-6th centuries CE.

Featuring Indian and Buddhist architecture, it functions as a Buddhist temple and pilgrimage site.

Notable structures include the Mahabodhi Temple itself and the sacred Bodhi Tree.

Tourists are drawn to its spiritual significance, historical importance, and architectural beauty.

Built by Emperor Ashoka and later expanded and renovated by various rulers, it symbolizes the birth of Buddhism and its spread across Asia.
