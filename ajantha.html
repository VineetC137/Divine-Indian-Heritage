Ajanta and Ellora Caves

The Ajanta and Ellora Caves, located in Aurangabad, Maharashtra, are UNESCO World Heritage Sites showcasing ancient Indian rock-cut architecture and art.

Ajanta Caves were established from the 2nd century BCE to 480 CE, while Ellora Caves were built from the 6th to 10th century CE.

These caves feature Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain cave temples and monasteries, representing diverse religious beliefs.

Functioning as religious and monastic complexes, they house intricate carvings, ancient paintings, and provide a spiritual ambiance.

Notable structures include Ajanta's Cave 1 (Maharaja's Palace) and Ellora's Kailasa Temple, which are marvels of ancient architecture.

Designated as UNESCO World Heritage Sites in 1983, they are protected and preserved by archaeological departments.
