Agra Fort

Agra Fort, located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, is an iconic Mughal fortress and UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Established in 1565, Agra Fort showcases Mughal architecture with its red sandstone walls.

Functioning as a military fortress and royal residence, it boasts notable structures like Jahangir Palace, Diwan-i-Khas, and Diwan-i-Am.

Tourists are drawn to its historical significance, museums, and panoramic views of the Taj Mahal.

Built by Emperor Akbar and expanded by his successors, Agra Fort witnessed key events in Indian history, including the imprisonment of Shah Jahan.

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983, it symbolizes Mughal power and grandeur.
