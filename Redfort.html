Red Fort (Lal Qila)

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a symbol of Mughal grandeur and architectural prowess, located in Delhi.

Established in 1648, the Red Fort boasts magnificent Mughal architecture with red sandstone walls.

Functioning as the residence of Mughal emperors and a ceremonial center, it holds significant historical importance.

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2007, the Red Fort is a major tourist destination in Delhi.

The Diwan-i-Am (Hall of Public Audience) and Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience) are notable structures within the fort.

Visitors are attracted to the sound and light show, museums, and the historical significance of the Red Fort.
