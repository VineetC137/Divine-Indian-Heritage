Hampi

Hampi, located in Karnataka, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and was once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire.

Established as an ancient city, Hampi dates back to the 14th century.

Its architecture primarily showcases Vijayanagara architecture, reflecting the grandeur of the empire.

Hampi served as the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire and was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1986.

Notable structures include the Virupaksha Temple and Vittala Temple, which are architectural marvels.

Visitors are attracted to its ruins, temples, and the unique boulder-strewn landscape.
